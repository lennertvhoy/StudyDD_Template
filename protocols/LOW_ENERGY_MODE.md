# LOW_ENERGY_MODE — Real Learner Sessions

> **Agent rule.** StudyDD supports real learners, not ideal learners.

## When To Use Low-Energy Mode

Choose low-energy mode when the learner:

- says they are tired
- says they have limited time
- asks for an easier session
- has completed several deep sessions recently
- seems distracted or low-bandwidth

## What To Do In Low-Energy Mode

1. Pick one due review item if available. See `protocols/SPACED_REPETITION_POLICY.md`.
2. If no due review, pick a small recall or explain question.
3. Keep feedback concise.
4. Do not ask hard scenario or design questions.
5. Do not inflate readiness.
6. Update state normally for the single item.

## What Not To Do In Low-Energy Mode

- Do not launch a long drill.
- Do not ask a hard question and then apologize for it.
- Do not move readiness up more than one small band.
- Do not skip evidence recording.

## Recovery Mode

Use recovery mode when the learner is stuck, frustrated, or wants to absorb without testing.

1. Let the learner pick a topic or choose the weakest skill.
2. Explain the concept plainly.
3. Optionally ask one gentle check-for-understanding question.
4. Do not grade harshly.
5. Do not upgrade readiness.
6. Record the session as a recovery/explanation entry.

## Permission

A low-energy or recovery session is still a valid session. There is no guilt. The goal is to keep the learner engaged without breaking the evidence discipline.
